An experiment: End Game & Spoilers

April 26, 2019: As of right now, everyone is in a frenzy about people needed to respect that there should be NO spoilers concerning End Game. While I understand the sentiment that over the course of 11 years, this one movie has a lot more going for it than the previous movies. It's a movie we've all have been waiting for the moment Infinity War ended. 


To put into context of where I stand, I've loved The Avengers since the first movie. It has a very special spot in my heart, and no other hero movie/character can get in the way of how I feel towards the members within the Avengers. 

I was around during the time Loki blew up on Tumblr. I was there with the hype, though my voice in the matter has been silenced. 
I keep my interest in the movies to a low, but I don't consider myself less of a fan for doing so.
 
I love these characters very much without having to be open as a lot of people. Perhaps I'm not the most factual person about the subject, but I have an emotional tie to this characters. 

With that being said, I'm just as happy to see all the posters, cosplaying, flags, attire surrounding the film. I'm just as happy to see them succeed like everyone else. I don't doubt that this will be a great movie.

So, I sought out the spoilers.


I took it upon myself to go and see key spoilers in the film because I firmly believe that if a movie is great, it will be despite the knowledge. People always evade me from spoilers as not to ruin the experience for me. It doesn't. It makes me want to watch it even more. 

The anticipation of wondering HOW it happens is what makes a movie to me, not what happens. I've done this to myself in the past which include Death Note and My Hero Academia. Spoilers may not technically be something I encourage to enjoy a film or a TV show or anything like that. However, it doesn't ruin my experience at all. If it was bad after being spoiled, it was just bad to begin with. If it's good after being spoiled, it's simply good.

With that being said, I'm still just as excited about End Game as I had been before. I will update this page when I'm able to get myself into a seat at the theater. ♡

--- 

April 30th, 2018: I saw End Game today. My time for the movie was at 5:00pm, and I just can't believe what a feeling it is to be a part of the crowd. I cried. I was on the edge. I clapped with the crowd. The last time I felt a part of the audience, cheering was back when I first saw The Avengers. It was a nostaglic feeling but a far, distant one all the same. I'm really happy with how things came to be. 

Admittedly, I thought there were somethings I'd have liked to see differently. Like my biggest glaring issue was just seeing how Thor was. His character was poor for a movie like this. However, I felt so much for this movie. I knew who wasn't going to make it in the end; I still cried for what happened. It was all about the what happened and how. The how is what keeps us wondering.  

I was excited for the little moments. I was thrilled for the major ones. The scene where all the girls bunched together with Captain Marvel? It was one of the first time I felt a nice connection to the feminist movement. It was the first time I thought to myself, wow girl power. Iconic. I was really happy to see Loki again even though his screen time was so minimal.
